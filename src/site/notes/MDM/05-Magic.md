# Magic

---

## Overview

Magic in MDM is **studied, fixed, and cinematic**. Mages are rare — roughly 1 in 100 people. When a mage casts a spell, it means something. The world notices. People fear it.

- Mages have a fixed spell list chosen at creation
- Spells cost **Spell Points (SP)** to cast
- Casting requires a **roll of 3d6 under IQ**
- Mages **cannot wear metal armor or use metal shields**
- SP recovers fully on rest

---

## Spell Points

**SP = IQ at creation.**

Casting a spell costs SP equal to the spell's listed cost. If you don't have enough SP, you can't cast it.

SP recovers fully after a full rest (a night's sleep or equivalent).

---

## Starting Spells

**At creation, a mage knows a number of spells equal to IQ minus 8.**

| IQ | Starting Spells |
|----|----------------|
| 10 | 2 |
| 11 | 3 |
| 12 | 4 |
| 13 | 5 |
| 14 | 6 |

You must meet the minimum IQ requirement for each spell you select. Additional spells can be learned through advancement (2 AP each) with access to a teacher or spell text.

---

## Casting a Spell

1. Declare the spell and target
2. Pay the SP cost
3. Roll 3d6 under IQ
4. Success = spell fires as described
5. Failure = spell fails, SP is still spent
6. Botch (17-18) = spell misfires — GM determines effect

## Magic in the World

Mages are rare. Roughly **1 in 100 people** have the ability. Most hide it.

In most settlements, a mage who reveals themselves publicly risks:
- Fear and suspicion from ordinary people
- Attention from those who want to exploit them
- Hostility from religious or political factions

There is no mage's guild in Varek. There is no licensing authority. There is only discretion, and the consequences of losing it.

> [!GM] Treat revealed magic as a social event, not just a mechanical one. When a mage casts in public, watch how NPCs react. That reaction is part of the world.
