# Races

---

## Overview

Races in MDM are human — or were. The Touched lineages are divergent strains, marked by generations of exposure to forces older than civilization. They look mostly human. They aren't, quite.

---

## Human

*The unmarked. The adaptable. The majority.*

No special origin. No ancient curse or blessing. Humans are everywhere precisely because they bend without breaking — traders, soldiers, scholars, criminals, kings.

- **Stat Mods:** None
- **Fate Points:** 2
- **Racial Ability — Adaptable:** At character creation, gain 1 extra skill point (6 total).

**World Standing:** Humans set the baseline. They run most cities, most governments, most guilds. Being human means no one looks twice — which is its own kind of power.

**Natural Enemies:** None by default. Though the Touched often blame humans for their marginalization, humans rarely think about the Touched enough to hate them deliberately. Indifference cuts its own way.

---

## Ashborn

*Touched by catastrophe. Descendants of survivors from a cataclysmic event — fire, war, something worse.*

Skin carries faint char patterns. Eyes are amber or red-flecked. They run hot, never feel cold, and tend toward short lives lived at full volume.

- **Stat Mods:** ST +1, LK -1
- **Fate Points:** 2
- **Racial Ability — Ember Blood:** Once per encounter, when reduced to 0 HP, make a WL roll. Success = stay at 1 HP. Fail = go down normally.

**World Standing:** Treated as survivors or omens depending on who you ask. Welcomed in rough-and-tumble places — mercenary companies, frontier towns, disaster zones. Viewed with unease in stable, comfortable societies who'd rather forget whatever made them.

**Natural Enemies:** Tidemarked. Fire and deep water don't mix philosophically or personally. Ashborn find Tidemarked eerie and manipulative; Tidemarked find Ashborn exhausting and reckless. Neither is entirely wrong.

---

## Tidemarked

*Touched by deep water — specifically, whatever lives at the bottom of it.*

Born near coasts or underground lakes where something old and patient dwells. Faint bioluminescence under the skin. Pupils dilate unnaturally wide in darkness. Animals distrust them. They don't mind.

- **Stat Mods:** IQ +1, WL +1, ST -1
- **Fate Points:** 2
- **Racial Ability — Still Water:** Immune to Fear spells and the Fear condition. NPCs make first impression disposition rolls at -1.

**World Standing:** Unnerving to most, useful to some. Sought out as navigators, diplomats, and spies — people who need someone who won't flinch. Coastal communities tolerate them. Inland settlements whisper.

**Natural Enemies:** Ashborn (mutual). Also unsettling to most animals — creature instinct reads them as wrong.

---

## Greyborn

*Touched by death. Clinically died at birth. Came back.*

Pale. Slow pulse. Don't dream. Undead ignore them unless attacked first. The living find them unsettling in ways they struggle to articulate.

- **Stat Mods:** WL +1, LK -1
- **Fate Points:** 2
- **Racial Ability — Threshold:** Once per session, automatically stabilize at 0 HP without a roll.

**World Standing:** Feared in superstitious regions, studied in academic ones. Priests argue about whether they're blessed or cursed — usually without asking the Greyborn in question. Most learn early to keep their nature quiet.

**Natural Enemies:** No specific Touched rivalry, but organized religion is frequently hostile. Any faction with a strong afterlife doctrine tends to view Greyborn as an affront — something that shouldn't have come back, and probably left something behind when it did.
