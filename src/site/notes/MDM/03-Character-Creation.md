# Character Creation

---

## Step by Step

### 1. Define Your Outsider
Every character is an **Outsider** who walked through a Door. Work with your GM using the co-build template to define your species.

You will then build out your **Outsider racial package** using:
- **Stat Modifiers** (from your species)
- **Advantages & Disadvantages** (racial traits and quirks)
- **Optional Powers** (magical/psionic abilities)

You automatically have the Outsider disadvantage (1pt or 2pt). See [[MDM/07-Races\|07-Races]]

### 2. Set Your Stats
All stats begin at **10**. You have **+3 stat points** to distribute as you choose.

**Point Economy:**
- You get +3 stat points to allocate freely
- You may also voluntarily reduce any stat down to 7 (minimum). Each point reduced grants 1 additional stat point to spend elsewhere.
  - Example: Reduce DX from 10 to 7 (−3) to gain 3 points. Put those 3 points into ST, raising it from 10 to 13.

**Limits at creation:**
- Minimum: 7 (can drop from 10 for points)
- Maximum: 14

### 3. Calculate HP
**HP = your final ST score.**

### 4. Assign Advantages & Disadvantages

Define your character's racial traits and quirks using advantages and disadvantages.

#### Advantage Points (AP) Economy

Advantages and disadvantages use a separate **Advantage Points (AP)** economy:
- All PCs start with **-1 or -2 AP** from the mandatory Outsider disadvantage
- You can take up to **-2 more AP in additional disadvantages** beyond Outsider
- Spend accumulated AP on advantages
- You always have **5 skill points** for skills (separate resource)

**Example:** Outsider (1 pt disadvantage) = 1 AP to spend. Take Frail (2 pt disadvantage) = -2 more AP, total 3 AP. Now you can buy Tough (2 pts) and Iron Will (1 pt).

#### Limits
- **Maximum 2 advantages** (standard or Powers, any mix)
- **Maximum 2 disadvantages** (not counting mandatory Outsider)
- **Powers advantages and Powers disadvantages are mutually exclusive** — you cannot take both
- **Maximum 3 quirks** (free, no AP cost)
- **Outsider disadvantage** (1-2 pts) is mandatory for all PCs and does not count toward disadvantage limit

#### Powers Advantages
*Determine your Power Points capacity. See Step 6.*

| Advantage           | AP Cost | Effect                                                                                                              |

#### Quirks
*Free. Flavor only. Maximum 3.*

No mechanical effect. Exist purely to give your character texture and roleplay hooks. May result in GM favor at their discretion.

- Talks to weapons or gear
- Never sleeps indoors
- Compulsive gambler (small stakes only)
- Hums when nervous
- Distrusts mages
- Collects trophies from kills
- Refuses to lie outright (but will mislead)
- Always names their horse
- Superstitious about a specific thing
- Has a specific food or drink they refuse to touch
- Never turns their back on a door
- Obsessively counts things under stress
- Kisses weapons goodnight before sleeping

### 5. Assign Skills

You have **5 skill points** to spend. Each skill costs 1 skill point.

- You may take any skill once at creation
- Untrained skills can still be attempted at **-2 to the linked stat**

#### How Skills Work

Skills represent training and practiced ability. When you attempt something covered by a skill:

1. Identify the linked stat
2. Roll 3d6 under that stat
3. If you have the skill: roll normally
4. If you don't have the skill: roll at **-2 to the linked stat**

#### Skill Notes

**Specialization:** At the GM's discretion, a character with a skill may claim a specialization (e.g., Lockpicking: Vault Locks) for a +1 bonus in that specific context.

**Opposed checks:** When two characters contest directly (e.g., Stealth vs. Perception), both roll. Lowest successful roll wins. Ties go to the defender.

**Combat skills:** Melee Combat, Ranged Combat, and Thrown Combat do not add to hit chance directly — they represent trained technique. The GM may award situational bonuses for skilled combatants in specific circumstances (e.g., disarming, called shots).

### 6. Calculate Power Points (Power Users)
**Power Points (PP) determine your capacity for Powers.** Powers include spells, psionic abilities, robotic tech, and other abilities that come naturally to your race.

**PP is determined by your Powers advantages:**
- **Powers Inclined (1 pt):** PP = half your IQ
- **Powers Invested (2 pts):** PP = your IQ

You must take one of these advantages to have any PP. If you take neither, you have 0 PP and cannot use Powers.

**Note:** You cannot take any Powers advantage AND any Powers disadvantage. See Step 4 above.

### 7. Choose Powers (Power Users Only)
If your character has Power Points, select Powers up to your IQ minimum. You must meet the IQ requirement for each Power.

Number of starting Powers = **IQ minus 8** (e.g. IQ 13 = 5 Powers). See [[MDM/04-Powers\|04-Powers]].

### 8. Choose Equipment
Select starting gear from the equipment list. The GM may provide a starting package or a budget.

**Default starting budget:** 10 gold pieces. See [[MDM/08-Equipment\|08-Equipment]].

### 9. Note Fate Points
All characters start with **2 Fate Points**.

### 10. Record Derived Values

| Value | Calculation |
|-------|-------------|
| HP | = ST |
| PP | Determined by Powers advantages (see Step 6) |
| Fate Points | 2 (all characters) |
| Initiative modifier | LK 13-14: +1 / LK 6-9: -1 / else: 0 |

## Quick Reference: Creation Checklist

-  Outsider species and abilities defined (via GM co-build)
-  Stats distributed (+3 stat points, min 7, max 14)
-  HP calculated (= ST)
-  Advantages & Disadvantages assigned
-  Skill points spent (5)
-  Power Points calculated (if taking Powers advantages)
-  Powers chosen (if taking Powers advantages)
-  Equipment selected
-  Fate Points noted (2)
-  Outsider disadvantage noted (1pt or 2pt)
