# Pre-Generated Characters

---

Six ready-to-play characters for Might, Daring & Magic. All built on standard creation rules with 1 advantage, 1 disadvantage, and 1 quirk each.

---

## 1. DAVAN CROSS — The Fighter
*Human | Competent, mercenary, uncomplicated about violence*

### Stats
| ST | DX | IQ | WL | LK | HP | FP |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| 13 | 11 | 10 | 10 | 9 | 15 | 2 |

### Point Breakdown
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Start (Human) | 6 |
| **Tough** (advantage) | -2 → 4 |
| **Code of Honor** (disadvantage) | +1 → 5 |
| Melee Combat | -1 → 4 |
| Athletics | -1 → 3 |
| Intimidation | -1 → 2 |
| Survival Wilderness | -1 → 1 |
| Perception | -1 → 0 |

### Advantage
- **Tough** — +2 HP permanently (HP is 15, not 13)

### Disadvantage
- **Code of Honor** — Won't strike a downed enemy, won't attack from ambush, won't take a job that targets civilians. GM invokes when it complicates things.

### Quirk
- *Never sleeps indoors if he can avoid it*

### Skills
- Melee Combat
- Athletics
- Intimidation
- Survival Wilderness
- Perception

### Equipment
- Broadsword (2d6, ST10)
- Chain armor (DR3, ST11)
- Round shield (-2 attacker DX, ST10)
- Dagger (1d6, backup)
- Bedroll, flint & steel
- Waterskin, 5 days rations
- Whetstone
- 3sp, 8cp

### Backstory
Davan spent eight years as a hired sword for a merchant coalition that no longer exists. He didn't cause its collapse — he just survived it, which is more than most of his colleagues managed. He has no strong allegiances, a functional sense of honor, and a mild drinking problem he'd describe as a preference. He came to Varek because someone owed him money. They're dead now. He stayed because leaving requires a destination.

**Personality hook:** Loyal to whoever's paying, but the Code of Honor means he's walked away from three jobs that paid well. Figuring out where his lines are is half the fun.

## 2. SABLE — The Mage
*Greyborn | Unsettling, precise, privately terrified of what she is*

### Stats
| ST | DX | IQ | WL | LK | HP | FP | SP |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|-----|
| 8 | 10 | 13 | 11 | 9 | 8 | 2 | 13 |

*(WL +1, LK -1 from Greyborn)*

### Point Breakdown
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Start | 5 |
| **Arcane Sensitivity** (advantage) | -1 → 4 |
| **Distinctive** (disadvantage) | +1 → 5 |
| Lore Arcane | -1 → 4 |
| Perception | -1 → 3 |
| Deception | -1 → 2 |
| Medicine | -1 → 1 |
| Stealth | -1 → 0 |

### Advantage
- **Arcane Sensitivity** — Can detect active magic without casting — vague sense only

### Disadvantage
- **Distinctive** — The pale skin, the slow pulse, the way undead ignore her. NPCs remember her. Disguise is nearly impossible.

### Quirk
- *Refuses to lie outright — will mislead, misdirect, omit, but won't say something she knows is false*

### Skills
- Lore Arcane
- Perception
- Deception
- Medicine
- Stealth

### Spells
*IQ 13 = 5 starting spells (IQ minus 8)*

| Spell | SP Cost | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Arcane Bolt | 1 | 1d6, ignores DR |
| Shield | 1 | +1 DR |
| Heal | 2 | 1d6 HP restored |
| Invisibility | 3 | Invisible until attack/cast |
| Frost Lance | 2 | 1d6+1 + DX loss |

### Racial Ability — Threshold
Once per session, automatically stabilize at 0 HP without a roll.

### Equipment
- Dagger (1d6)
- Heavy cloak
- Spell journal (encrypted personal cipher)
- Ink, quill, 6 sheets parchment
- Small bronze mirror
- Waterskin, 3 days rations
- Lockbox (empty, but locked)
- 1sp, 4cp

### Backstory
Sable died for eleven seconds at birth. Her mother counted. Whatever came back wore her daughter's face and grew up knowing something was wrong with her — not broken, just offset. She discovered she could work magic at fourteen, which in a world that fears mages meant she learned to keep her mouth shut before she learned to shave. She's been careful ever since. She joined this group because they don't ask many questions, and because she's running low on places to hide.

**Personality hook:** Clinically calm under pressure, visibly uncomfortable with kindness. Undead ignore her. She pretends not to notice.

*Ashborn | Reckless, warm, spectacularly bad at long-term planning*

### Stats
| ST | DX | IQ | WL | LK | HP | FP |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| 11 | 13 | 10 | 9 | 11 | 11 | 2 |

*(ST +1, LK -1 from Ashborn)*

### Point Breakdown
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Start | 5 |
| **Light Sleeper** (advantage) | -1 → 4 |
| **Impulsive** (disadvantage) | +1 → 5 |
| Ranged Combat | -1 → 4 |
| Stealth | -1 → 3 |
| Acrobatics | -1 → 2 |
| Survival Wilderness | -1 → 1 |
| Perception | -1 → 0 |

### Advantage
- **Light Sleeper** — Always wakes at danger, never caught asleep on watch

### Disadvantage
- **Impulsive** — Must make WL roll (3d6 under 9) to hold back when action is possible

### Quirk
- *Hums when nervous — usually in combat*

### Racial Ability — Ember Blood
Once per encounter, when reduced to 0 HP, make a WL roll. Success = stay at 1 HP. Fail = go down normally.

### Skills
- Ranged Combat
- Stealth
- Acrobatics
- Survival Wilderness
- Perception

### Equipment
- Short bow (1d6+1, ST8), 24 arrows
- Hand axe (1d6+1, throwable, backup)
- Leather armor (DR1)
- Rope 15m
- Grappling hook
- Backpack, bedroll, flint & steel
- Waterskin, 4 days rations
- Spare bowstring
- Knife for camp use (not combat)
- 2sp, 2cp

### Backstory
Rynn grew up in a settlement built on the edge of where something burned badly enough to leave scars in the ground. Ashborn communities tend to cluster near old disasters — she never questioned it until she left and realized most people find that strange. She took work as a courier, then a scout, then a person who gets paid to go places other people won't. She's cheerful about danger in a way that either reassures people or deeply unsettles them. No in-between.

**Personality hook:** Ember Blood has saved her life twice. Light Sleeper means she's always first up. Impulsive means she's usually already moving before anyone else has a plan.

## 4. TORVEN — The Face
*Human | Silver-tongued, genuinely kind, hiding a specific past*

### Stats
| ST | DX | IQ | WL | LK | HP | FP |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| 10 | 11 | 11 | 13 | 12 | 10 | 2 |

### Point Breakdown
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Start (Human) | 6 |
| **Intimidating Presence** (advantage) | -1 → 5 |
| **Enemies** (disadvantage, 1pt) | +1 → 6 |
| Persuasion | -1 → 5 |
| Deception | -1 → 4 |
| Leadership | -1 → 3 |
| Survival Urban | -1 → 2 |
| Sleight of Hand | -1 → 1 |
| Perception | -1 → 0 |

### Advantage
- **Intimidating Presence** — First social roll against a new NPC at +1 (easier). Combined with LK 12, strangers almost always warm to him immediately.

### Disadvantage
- **Enemies** (1pt) — The senior partner from his old consortium has connections and a long memory. Someone's been asking questions about him in Varek.

### Quirk
- *Compulsive gambler — small stakes only, but always*

### Skills
- Persuasion
- Deception
- Leadership
- Survival Urban
- Sleight of Hand
- *(+1 Human bonus):* Perception

### Equipment
- Short sword (1d6+1)
- Leather armor (DR1)
- Fine clothes (worn over armor in social situations)
- Loaded dice (sleight of hand prop)
- Forged letter of introduction (merchant house, dissolved)
- Backpack, bedroll
- Waterskin, 3 days rations
- Small notebook, charcoal pencil
- Flask of decent wine
- 6sp (looks like more)

### Backstory
Torven was, briefly, legitimately employed as a negotiator for a mid-tier trading consortium. He was good at it. Then the consortium's senior partner discovered Torven had been quietly redirecting a percentage of every deal into a fund for workers the consortium had stiffed — nothing dramatic, just math applied with a conscience. He was let go. Politely. With a threat attached. He's been freelance since, which suits him fine. He's one of those rare people who actually likes other people, which makes him either the most trustworthy person in the room or the most dangerous, depending on your read.

**Personality hook:** High LK and Intimidating Presence mean strangers warm to him immediately. The Enemies disadvantage means someone from his past is getting closer. He knows it. He's waiting for the other shoe.

## 5. MIRA VOSS — The Thief
*Tidemarked | Quiet, observant, professionally invisible*

### Stats
| ST | DX | IQ | WL | LK | HP | FP |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| 8 | 13 | 12 | 11 | 10 | 8 | 2 |

*(ST -1, IQ +1, WL +1 from Tidemarked)*

### Point Breakdown
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Start | 5 |
| **Lucky Break** (advantage) | -2 → 3 |
| **Greedy** (disadvantage) | +1 → 4 |
| Lockpicking | -1 → 3 |
| Stealth | -1 → 2 |
| Sleight of Hand | -1 → 1 |
| Trap Handling | -1 → 0 |
| *(Perception — untrained, -2 penalty)* | — |

### Advantage
- **Lucky Break** — Once per session, reroll any single die and take either result

### Disadvantage
- **Greedy** — Must make WL roll (3d6 under 11) to pass up significant treasure or payment

### Quirk
- *Always kiss there weapons good night*

### Racial Ability — Still Water
Immune to Fear spells and the Fear condition. NPCs make first impression disposition rolls at -1.

### Skills
- Lockpicking
- Stealth
- Sleight of Hand
- Trap Handling

### Equipment
- Dagger (1d6)
- Quality lockpicks
- Leather armor (DR1)
- Dark clothing (head to toe)
- Small mirror on a wire (door gap viewer)
- Chalk x3, string 10m, 6 iron spikes
- Waterskin, 3 days rations
- Thin pry bar
- Small sack (for carrying things out)
- 4sp

### Backstory
Mira doesn't talk about where she's from, and the Tidemarked bioluminescence under her skin makes it hard to pretend she's from anywhere normal. She works clean — no violence unless necessary, no witnesses if possible, no jobs that feel personal. She ended up in Varek the way most people do: running from something, not toward anything. The Greedy disadvantage isn't pure avarice — it's that she grew up with nothing and the sight of something valuable triggers a deep, irrational need to have it.

**Personality hook:** Lucky Break fits her uncanny knack for things working out at the last second. Greedy is the thing that gets her into situations where she needs it.

*Human | Loud, loyal, picks fights he can almost win*

### Stats
| ST | DX | IQ | WL | LK | HP | FP |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| 13 | 11 | 9 | 10 | 11 | 15 | 2 |

### Point Breakdown
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Start (Human) | 6 |
| **Tough** (advantage) | -2 → 4 |
| **Combat Reflexes** (advantage) | -2 → 2 |
| **Cowardly** (disadvantage) | +1 → 3 |
| Melee Combat | -1 → 2 |
| Athletics | -1 → 1 |
| Intimidation | -1 → 0 |

### Advantages
- **Tough** — +2 HP permanently (HP is 15, not 13)
- **Combat Reflexes** — +1 initiative always, never surprised

### Disadvantage
- **Cowardly** — Must make WL roll (3d6 under 10) to engage in clearly losing fights

### Quirk
- *Never turns his back on a door*

### Skills
- Melee Combat
- Athletics
- Intimidation

### Equipment
- Battle Axe (2d6+1, ST11)
- Chain armor (DR3, ST11)
- Dagger (1d6, backup)
- Backpack, bedroll, flint & steel
- Waterskin, 4 days rations
- Whetstone
- Flask (usually empty)
- 2sp, 5cp

### Backstory
Brek ran the door at three different taverns before getting fired from all three for escalating situations he was supposed to de-escalate. He's not stupid — he just has a finely tuned sense of which fights are worth having and an unfortunate tendency to have them anyway. The Cowardly disadvantage isn't cowardice exactly — it's that Brek has excellent situational awareness and genuinely doesn't want to die. He just usually misjudges what "losing" looks like until it's too late.

**Personality hook:** Combat Reflexes means he's always first to act — and Cowardly means he sometimes immediately regrets it.
