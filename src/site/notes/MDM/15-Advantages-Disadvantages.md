# Advantages, Disadvantages & Quirks

---

## Overview

This system is **optional**. Use it at character creation to add mechanical texture and personality to your character.

Advantages and disadvantages use the same **skill point pool** as skills (starting pool: 5 points, +1 if Human). Quirks are free flavor with no mechanical effect.

**Limits:**
- Maximum 2 advantages
- Maximum 2 disadvantages
- Maximum 3 quirks
- Net skill point gain from disadvantages capped at +2 (can't farm disadvantages for extra skills)

---

## Advantages
*Cost skill points at creation.*

| Advantage | Cost | Effect |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **Combat Reflexes** | 2 | +1 initiative always, never surprised. May move to Close as a free action on the first round of combat. |
| **Tough** | 2 | +2 HP permanently |
| **Lucky Break** | 2 | Once per session, reroll any single die and take either result |
| **Iron Will** | 1 | +1 WL vs magic and fear effects |
| **Light Sleeper** | 1 | Always wakes at danger — never caught asleep on watch |
| **Quick Healer** | 2 | Recover +1 HP per rest |
| **Streetwise** | 1 | Treat Survival Urban as trained even if untrained |
| **Intimidating Presence** | 1 | First social roll against a new NPC at +1 DX (easier) |
| **Arcane Sensitivity** | 1 | Can detect active magic without a spell — vague sense only |
| **Hard to Kill** | 2 | Bleed out at -ST-3 instead of -ST |

---

## Disadvantages
*Refund skill points at creation.*

| Disadvantage | Refund | Effect |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| **Cowardly** | 1 | Must make WL roll to engage in clearly losing fights |
| **Distinctive** | 1 | Memorable appearance — disguise nearly impossible, NPCs always remember you |
| **Greedy** | 1 | Must make WL roll to pass up significant treasure or payment |
| **Bad Luck** | 2 | LK treated as 2 points lower for all passive threshold checks |
| **Lame** | 2 | Always acts last in initiative. Can only move one range band per turn — never two. Cannot move and act in the same turn if moving to Close. |
| **Illiterate** | 1 | Cannot read any language — no lore skills usable without a reader |
| **Enemies** | 1-2 | Someone is actively hunting or opposing you (GM defines scope) |
| **Impulsive** | 1 | Must make WL roll to delay or plan when action is possible |
| **Frail** | 2 | -2 HP permanently |
| **Code of Honor** | 1 | Strict personal code — GM can invoke to complicate decisions |

---

## Quirks
*Free. Flavor only. Maximum 3.*

No mechanical effect. Exist purely to give your character texture and roleplay hooks.

- Talks to weapons or gear
- Never sleeps indoors
- Compulsive gambler (small stakes only)
- Hums when nervous
- Distrusts mages
- Collects trophies from kills
- Refuses to lie outright (but will mislead)
- Always names their horse
- Superstitious about a specific thing
- Has a specific food or drink they refuse to touch
- Never turns their back on a door
- Obsessively counts things under stress
- Kisses weapons goodnight before sleeping

## Example Build

*Rynn Ashvale (Scout) with advantages/disadvantages applied:*

Starting pool: 5 points
- Takes **Combat Reflexes** (2 points) — fits her aggressive style
- Takes **Impulsive** (+1 refund) — she absolutely would
- Remaining for skills: 4 points
- Quirk: *Hums when nervous* (free)

> [!GM] Disadvantages should come up in play. If a player takes Impulsive and it never causes problems, you're not using it. Flag these at session start and invoke them at dramatically appropriate moments — not to punish players, but to make the character feel real.

---

## Literacy Note

All characters are assumed to be literate by default. The **Illiterate** disadvantage represents the exception — characters who grew up without access to written language. This is a disadvantage, not a skill. No roll is required to read; you either can or you can't.
