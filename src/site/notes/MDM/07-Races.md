# Races & Outsiders

---

## Overview

Nearly everyone in this world is human. Born here, raised here, died here. The world is medieval in feel — farms, cities, swords, dirt, politics, religion. 

And then...there are the Doors.

---

## The Doors

No one knows where they come from (or at least, no one you know). No one controls them (probably). They appear without warning — in alleyways, in fields, in the middle of forests — and they stay open for anywhere from a few minutes to a few days. Then they close. They do not reopen.

Almost everyone has heard of them. Most people know someone who knows someone who once saw one. A handful of people have walked through one — either by accident or by choice — and found themselves here, in a world that is not their own.

Those people are called **Outsiders**.

**What is known about Doors:**
- They appear randomly — no pattern to location, timing, or frequency
- They connect to other worlds entirely — different histories, different physics, different species. 
- Walking through is one-way. The Door closes. There is no going back unless the GM moves the entire party through a new one.
- Governments track them. Most have standing orders to locate and bring in anyone who comes through — to help them, study them, or use them. Usually some combination of all three.


---

## Playing an Outsider

Outsiders can be **anything from any world** — a Klingon warrior, a Tolkien elf, a synthetic android, a wizard from a high-magic setting, a soldier from a near-future war. The MDM stat system runs underneath everything. The origin world provides the flavor.

**All Outsiders have the Outsider disadvantage built in at creation.** This is not optional — it is the cost of being from somewhere else. The level (1pt or 2pt) is determined by how alien the character appears to the native population.

| Outsider Level | Refund | Who It Fits |
|----------------|--------|-------------|
| **1pt** | 1 | Looks mostly human, passes at a glance. Accent, customs, or knowledge gaps give them away on closer inspection. *Elves, near-humans, humans from other worlds.* |
| **2pt** | 2 | Visibly, unmistakably not from here. Rooms go quiet. Children stare. Animals react. *Klingons, androids, non-humanoid beings, anything with obvious alien biology.* |

## The Co-Build Template

When a player chooses to play an Outsider, the player and GM build the character together using this template. The goal is to translate the source material into MDM mechanics — not to simulate it perfectly, but to capture the feel.

### Step 1 — Describe the Origin
In plain language: who is this character and where are they from? What does their species/type look like? What are they known for?

### Step 2 — Define Racial Ability
One innate ability that reflects something meaningful about the species. It should be:
- Flavorful and specific to the origin
- Usable but not dominant — it should feel like a trait, not a superpower
- Described in MDM mechanical terms. 
	- Use Advantages/Disadvantages, Powers, and STAT modification to create a racial template for that Outsider. 
	  
	  

### Step 3 — Assign Outsider Level
GM and player agree on 1pt or 2pt based on how alien the character appears to native humans.

### Step 4 — Define 2-3 Flavor Traits
Non-mechanical descriptors that help the player roleplay the character. These are not rules — they are anchors.

## Example Templates
### Elf Mage
**Outsider:** 1pt — passes as human at a glance; disposition -1 in unfamiliar communities  
**Stats:** All 10 base +3
**Advantages:** Powers Invested (PP = IQ, 5 starting Powers)  
**Disadvantages:** Outsider 1pt


---

### Klingon Warrior

**Outsider:** 2pt — immediately identifiable; disposition -2 always, -3 in hostile communities  
**Stats:** All 10 base +3
**Advantages:** Combat Reflexes (+1 initiative, never surprised, free Close move round 1)  
**Disadvantages:** Outsider 2pt, Code of Honor 1pt  


---

### The Noir Detective
_Human | World-weary private investigator_

**Outsider:** 1pt — passes as human; carries an air of grim knowingness that makes people uneasy. Disposition -1 with authority figures (cops, officials).

|ST|DX|IQ|WL|LK|HP|FP|PP|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|10|10|13|10|10|10|2|0|

**Advantages:**

- **Sharp Eye** — Re-roll one failed Perception or Lore check per scene

**Disadvantages:**

- **Outsider (1pt)** — Something's off about them
- **Vice (1pt)** — Drinking, smoking, gambling; GM can invoke to complicate situations

**Skills:** Investigation (IQ), Perception (IQ), Streetwise (IQ), Persuasion (WL), Brawling (DX)

**Equipment:** Revolver, trench coat, flask, lockpicks, small notepad

---

### The Adventurer Archaeologist

_Human | Brains-and-brawn relic hunter_

**Outsider:** 1pt — passes as human; reputation precedes them. Disposition +1 with scholars, -1 with criminal fences and corrupt officials.

|ST|DX|IQ|WL|LK|HP|FP|PP|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|12|11|12|10|10|12|2|0|

**Advantages:**

- **Resourceful** — Once per session, improvise a solution with whatever's at hand (GM must allow a creative approach)

**Disadvantages:**

- **Outsider (1pt)** — Foreigner everywhere they go
- **Phobia (1pt)** — Pick one (snakes, heights, enclosed spaces); GM can invoke

**Skills:** Lore Ancient Civilizations (IQ), Athletics (ST), Brawling (DX), Perception (IQ), Persuasion (WL)

**Equipment:** Whip or revolver, leather jacket, satchel, rope, field journal
