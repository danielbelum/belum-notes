# Powers

---

## Overview

Powers are special abilities that come from your character's origin. They might be:
- **Spells** — studied magic from a high-magic world
- **Psionic Abilities** — innate mental powers
- **Robotic/Tech Powers** — built-in capabilities or tools
- **Racial Powers** — natural abilities from your species

All Powers are **studied and fixed** — you choose them at creation and learn more through advancement.

- You must have Power Points (PP) from taking **Powers Inclined** or **Powers Invested** advantage
- Powers cost **Power Points (PP)** to use
- Using a Power requires a **roll of 3d6 under IQ** (unless the Power says otherwise)
- PP recovers on rest or meditation

> [!NOTE] If your character does not take **Powers Inclined** or **Powers Invested**, you have 0 PP and cannot use any Powers.

---

## Power Points

**PP is determined by your Powers advantages at creation:**
- **Powers Inclined (1 pt advantage):** PP = half your IQ
- **Powers Invested (2 pt advantage):** PP = your full IQ

Using a Power costs PP equal to the Power's listed cost. If you don't have enough PP, you can't use it.

### PP Recovery

For powers such as Magic or Psionics or Solar powered robots, see below. But! Solar powered robots need the sun! 

| Method | PP Recovered |
|--------|-------------|
| 15 minutes of uninterrupted meditation | 1 PP |
| Full rest (night's sleep or equivalent) | Full PP |

Meditation requires quiet and stillness — not possible in combat or while travelling. You must be able to sit undisturbed. One meditation session per hour maximum.

**HP recovery** works the same as for all characters. See [[MDM/02-Core-Rules\|02-Core-Rules]] for the full healing rules. PP and HP recover independently.

---

## Starting Powers

**At creation, you know a number of Powers equal to IQ minus 8.**

| IQ | Starting Powers |
|----|----------------|
| 10 | 2 |
| 11 | 3 |
| 12 | 4 |
| 13 | 5 |
| 14 | 6 |

You must meet the minimum IQ requirement for each Power you select. Additional Powers can be learned through advancement (2 AP each, or 3 AP for Rare Powers) with access to a teacher or source material. Rare Powers cannot be selected at creation under any circumstances.

---

## Using a Power

1. Declare the Power and target
2. Pay the PP cost
3. Roll 3d6 under IQ (unless the Power specifies otherwise)
4. Success = Power activates as described
5. Failure = Power fails, PP is still spent
6. Botch (17-18) = Power misfires — GM determines effect

## Rare Powers

Rare Powers cannot be selected at character creation under any circumstances. They require a teacher or source material to learn and cost **3 AP** each (vs 2 AP for standard Powers). Most are known only to a handful of people in the world — if that many.

> [!GM] Finding a teacher for a Rare Power should be an adventure in itself, not a shopping trip. The Power exists. Finding someone who knows it and will teach it is the story.

## Powers in the World

Powers are uncommon. Most people never encounter them. Those who have them often keep quiet about it.

In most settlements, someone with Powers who reveals them publicly risks:
- Fear and suspicion from ordinary people
- Attention from those who want to exploit or control them
- Hostility from religious, political, or military factions

There is no unified authority regulating Powers in Varek. There is no licensing. There is only discretion, and the consequences of losing it.
