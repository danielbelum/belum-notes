# Playtest Scenario: The Duskbell Job

*A one-session scenario for 3-4 players set in Varek.*

---

## Overview

A minor fence named **Corren Dusk** has gone missing. His associate — whoever hired the PCs — wants to know why, and wants back a lockbox Corren was holding. Simple retrieval job. Except Corren's last known location is a half-collapsed tavern in the old district now occupied by a small gang calling themselves the Ashpike, and the lockbox apparently has something in it that's making people nervous.

**This scenario tests:** Social negotiation, skill rolls, a mid-size combat, a boss encounter, LK as passive mechanic, Fate Point moments, and magic used by both PCs and enemies.

---

## What's Actually Going On

> [!GM] Read this before running. Don't share it with players.

Corren stumbled onto a mage — a rare, unlicensed one — hiding in the ruins beneath the tavern. The mage, **Sela**, isn't dangerous by intent. She's exhausted, scared, and has been bartering minor magic for protection from the Ashpike. The lockbox contains proof of her existence — letters, a crude spell journal. Corren got cold feet and the Ashpike locked him in the cellar to keep him quiet while they figure out what Sela is worth.

The Ashpike leader, **Brenn**, is pragmatic. He hasn't decided whether to sell Sela, use her, or cut her loose. He's running out of time to decide.

---

## Structure

### Act 1 — The Hook
*Tests: Social mechanics, disposition, partial information*

**Scene: The Hiring**

The PCs are approached by **Maret**, a nervous trader who works the Claim market. Corren owed her money and held goods on her behalf — including the lockbox. She doesn't know what's in it. She just wants it back and wants to know if Corren is alive.

- Offers 5sp upfront, 10sp on delivery
- Hiding something minor — suspects contraband, doesn't know it's magic
- **Perception or Detect Lies** success: she's not telling everything
- **Persuasion** success: she admits she thinks it might be "something illegal, maybe smuggled goods"

---

### Act 2 — Finding the Ashpike
*Tests: Skills, LK as passive threshold, light social*

**Scene: The Old District**

The Ashpike operate out of a tavern called **The Split Beam** — half the roof is gone, the bar still runs.

- No roll needed to find it — any Claim local knows the Ashpike's turf
- **Survival Urban** success: reveals a back entrance through a collapsed alley
- **LK check on arrival:** 10+ = door guard is distracted. 9 or below = PCs are clocked immediately.

**At the door:** Two Ashpike mooks. Not immediately hostile.

- **Persuasion or Deception** to talk their way in (disposition: neutral, -1 if any Tidemarked in the party)
- Valid angles: buyers, debt collectors, Corren's associates
- Failure = told to leave. Pushback escalates.

---

### Act 3 — Inside the Split Beam
*Tests: Full social mechanic suite, partial success/failure, player-driven negotiation*

**Scene: Meeting Brenn**

**Brenn** sits at the back of the bar eating. Four mooks visible. Two more below with Corren and Sela.

**Brenn's stats:** ST 12 | DX 11 | IQ 10 | WL 11 | LK 10 | HP 12
Disposition: Neutral (0). Pragmatic. Doesn't want unnecessary trouble.

**Social beats:**
- Asking about Corren: Brenn denies — **Deception roll at +1** (he's practiced)
- Offering money for the box: Brenn gets interested, asks what's in it
- Threatening: Brenn laughs. Then his mooks stand up.
- **Perception** (difficulty -1): Notice cellar door behind bar, faint sounds below

**Leverage point:** If players know about Sela, they can shift the negotiation entirely. Brenn doesn't want anyone coming looking for a mage on his turf. That changes his calculus.

**Possible outcomes:**
- Deal struck: Box returned, Corren produced (roughed up, alive)
- Partial deal: Box yes, Corren costs extra
- Breakdown: Combat (Act 4)

---

### Act 4 — The Fight
*Tests: Full combat — initiative, DX rolls, damage, armor, shields, weapons, 0 HP/bleed, boss mechanics, enemy spellcasting*

**Triggered by:** Negotiation breakdown, sneaking in through the back, or players kicking the door in.

---

#### The Ashpike Mooks (x6)
ST 9 | DX 9 | IQ 8 | HP 9
Armor: Leather (DR1)
Weapon: Short Sword (1d6+1) or Hand Axe (1d6+1)
*Act as two groups of three*

---

#### Brenn — Boss
ST 12 | DX 11 | IQ 10 | WL 11 | LK 10 | HP 12
Armor: Chain (DR3)
Weapon: Broadsword (2d6)
**Special — Read the Room:** Once per combat, Brenn may delay his action to interrupt a PC's declared action before they roll.

---

#### Kael — Ashpike Mage (Hidden Asset)
> [!GM] Kael is Brenn's hole card. He starts hidden on the upper floor (what's left of it), watching through a gap in the floorboards. He acts on Round 2, announcing himself with a Lightning Strike from above.

ST 8 | DX 10 | IQ 12 | WL 10 | LK 9 | HP 8 | SP 10
Armor: None (no metal — players may clock this as a tell)
Weapon: Dagger (1d6, last resort)

**Spells:**
| Spell | SP | Effect |
|-------|----|--------|
| Lightning Strike | 2 | 2d6, single target |
| Blinding Flash | 2 | -3 DX to target |
| Shield | 1 | +1 DR (pre-cast Round 1) |
| Frost Lance | 2 | 1d6+1 + DX loss |

**Tactics:**
- Round 1: Hidden, casts Shield on himself (players don't see this yet)
- Round 2: Reveals with Lightning Strike on the most armored PC
- Round 3+: Blinding Flash on anyone closing, Frost Lance to kite
- Below 4 SP: Falls back to dagger, tries to flee through the roof gap

> [!GM] Kael is scared of what he is. He and Sela know each other by rumor only. If players capture rather than kill him, that's a thread. If Sela hears his name, she reacts.

---

#### Terrain
- Half the roof is gone — dim light: -1 to ranged attacks
- The bar provides cover (+1 DR if crouching behind it)
- Cellar door: opening it as an action lets Corren shout up — all enemies who fail a WL roll lose their next initiative

**Fate Point moment:** If a PC goes down hard, this is a natural spot to award a Fate Point to another player for a heroic response.

---

### Act 5 — The Cellar
*Tests: Magic-as-rare theme, moral decision-making, player values, Fate Point potential*

**Scene: Finding Sela**

Corren is tied up — alive, scared, grateful. Sela is in the corner, hood up, visibly exhausted.

She's a mage. She'll hide it.
- **Perception or Lore Arcane** success: faint smell of spent magic, the way she flinches

**Sela — NPC, not a combatant**
IQ 13 | WL 9 | SP: 2 remaining
Spells: Heal, Light, Unlock, Arcane Bolt (won't use offensively unless cornered)
She's been hiding in Varek three months. Not registered. Not dangerous. Terrified.

**The choice:**
- Turn her in (to who? There's no authority in Varek)
- Let her go
- Help her
- Use her as leverage

The lockbox contents confirm she's a mage — crude spell journal, letters. Maret didn't know. What do the players do with that?

---

## Wrap

**Maret pays** if the box is returned. She doesn't open it in front of them. Whether she eventually discovers what's inside is a GM call for future sessions.

**Corren** is grateful, broke, and now owes the PCs a favor.

**Brenn**, if alive, remembers. Fair dealing = future contact. Burned = long memory.

**Sela** is a thread. Pull it when ready.

---

## Playtest Checklist

> [!PLAYTEST] Track these during the session

-  Does 3d6 under DX feel right for hit rates?
-  Is damage output too high / too low vs. HP totals?
-  Do Fate Points get used or hoarded?
-  Does LK as passive threshold feel noticeable?
-  Do social rolls feel meaningful or like speed bumps?
-  Does the mage feel appropriately rare and impactful?
-  Is the boss fight a good difficulty spike?
-  Did 0 HP / bleed rules create tension?
-  Did encumbrance come up? Was it annoying?
-  Did players understand the untrained skill penalty without being told?
-  Do enemy spell tactics feel distinct from weapon combat?
-  Is SP depletion a meaningful resource pressure on enemy mages?
