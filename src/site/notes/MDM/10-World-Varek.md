# The World — Varek

---

## Overview

*A city that should be dead. Isn't.*

Varek was burned — or flooded, or cursed, depending on who you ask and how drunk they are. The old districts are half-collapsed, streets choked with rubble, basements open to the sky. But the bones are good. The walls mostly stand. The river still runs through it.

That's enough. People came back.

Not the original inhabitants — they're gone, no one talks about where. But settlers, scavengers, opportunists, and desperate folk have been trickling in for two generations now. What's emerging is rough, loud, and alive in the way only places with nothing to lose can be.

There's no single authority in Varek. There are factions with reach, personalities with power, and enough competing interests to keep any one of them from locking it down. That tension is the city.

---

## Magic in Varek

There is no mage's guild in Varek. There is no licensing authority. Mages who end up here are usually hiding, running, or out of options. The city doesn't hunt them — it doesn't have the organization — but individual reactions range from fear to exploitation.

Roughly 1 in 100 people in the world can work magic. In Varek, the ratio is probably higher. People who need to disappear end up in places like this.

---

## Tone

Varek is **sword & sorcery** — pulpy, dangerous, and alive with possibility. It is not a hopeless grimdark ruin, nor a comfortable fantasy city. It's a place where fortunes get made and lost fast, where allegiances shift, and where the smart money is always on getting out before things go wrong.

The wrong things have already gone wrong once. Everyone in Varek knows it. Most of them are betting it won't happen again.

---

> [!GM] Varek is intentionally underdeveloped at this stage. Build it as you play. The Duskbell Job scenario gives you a neighborhood (the Claim), a gang (the Ashpike), a tavern (the Split Beam), and several NPCs to start from. Let the city grow outward from what the players touch.
