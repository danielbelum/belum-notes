# Advancement

---

## Overview

Characters advance through a combination of **sessions played** and **milestones** — significant story moments the GM designates. Advancement requires narrative justification: you must have had opportunity to practice, study, or experience what you're improving.

The GM awards **Advancement Points (AP)** at the end of sessions or after milestones.

---

## Advancement Costs

| Improvement | AP Cost | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| New Skill | 2 | Must have had opportunity to practice |
| Stat +1 (up to 12) | 3 | Requires justification |
| Stat +1 (13-14) | 5 | Requires significant study/training |
| Stat +1 (15-16) | 8 | Exceptional — rare, requires extended effort |
| New Spell | 2 | Must meet IQ minimum, requires a teacher or text |
| +2 SP | 2 | Reflects deepening magical stamina |

---

## Justification Requirement

You cannot simply spend points. Each advancement must make narrative sense.

**Examples of valid justification:**
- Improving Athletics after a session involving significant climbing or running
- Learning a new spell after studying with a mage or finding a spell text
- Raising IQ after extended research or study between sessions
- Gaining a new skill after training with an expert

**The GM decides** whether justification is sufficient. When in doubt, ask before the session ends.

---

## Worked Example

*Rynn (Ashborn Scout) completed a session that involved a long river crossing, tracking through the wilderness, and a close-range fight where her bow was nearly useless.*

At session end, the GM awards **3 AP**.

Rynn's player wants to:
- Improve Swimming (she nearly drowned — 2 AP, justified ✓)
- Add Melee Combat skill (she fought up close all session — 2 AP, justified ✓)

That's 4 AP total. She only has 3. She takes Swimming now and saves 1 AP toward Melee Combat next session.

---

## Advancement Caps

- No stat may exceed **16** through advancement
- Stats above 14 represent genuinely exceptional individuals
- Stats above 16 are reserved for monsters and legendary figures

> [!GM] Don't let advancement outpace the fiction. A character who raises ST to 16 should feel like they've earned something over many sessions, not just spent points.
